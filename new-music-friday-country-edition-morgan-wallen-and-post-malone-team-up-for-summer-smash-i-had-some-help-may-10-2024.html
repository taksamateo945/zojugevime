Morgan Wallen and Post Malone Team Up For Summer Smash I Had Some Help May 10, 2024 Ce

Posted by Reinaldo Massengill on Thursday, January 25, 2024

Post Malone and Morgan Wallen are unstoppable.

The fiery, tongue-in-cheek breakup collaboration admits fault from both parties in the relationship and is soon to be the next chart-topper.

Throughout the week, the hip-hop artist has been teasing this collaboration with Wallen posting a photo arm-in-arm with the caption: “They say teamwork makes the dream work.”

they say teamwork makes the dream work @MorganWallen pic.twitter.com/rEy79hCDp1

— Post Malone (@PostMalone) April 25, 2024

The “Circles” singer has been teasing his transition into country music over the last few months – a transition that have left some puzzled – but it’s clear that Post Malone has a knack and a love for the genre. He dropped a cover of Brad Paisley’s “I’m Gonna Miss Her” and recently found a home on Beyoncé’s song “Levi’s Jean,” featured on her own country debut, Cowboy Carter.

Aside from perhaps the biggest collaboration of the year, more of today’s top artists and country risers dropped brand new full-length projects that we are absolutely obsessed with that made this week’s New Music Friday playlist – Scotty McCreery, Brian Kelley, Lily Rose, Wade Bowen, Orville Peck, Peytan Porter, Kelsey Waldon and Terri Clark.

But that’s not all. Many more of our favorites released soon-to-be hit singles, including Thomas Rhett’s “Beautiful As You,” Carly Pearce’s “fault line,” Reba McEntire’s “I Can’t,” Chase Rice’s “Fireside,” Koe Wetzel’s “9 Lives,” Tigirlily Gold’s “Leroy,” Danielle Bradbery’s “Wedding or a Funeral,” Avery Anna’s “Blonde,” Mitchell Tenpenny’s “Not Today,” Matt Stell’s “Built by Broken Hearts,” The War and Treaty’s “Leads Me Home,” Kylie Morgan’s “Scratching The Surface,” Georgia Webster’s “This Ain’t A Breakup,” Aidan Canfield’s “Sore,” Ray Fulcher’s “Daytona To Me,” Cooper Alan’s “Feel Like Hell Today,” Jake Kohn‘s “Where Do We Go From Here?,” Drake White‘s “10 Lbs of S#!T,” Willow Avalon‘s “Hey There, Dolly,”  Jagger Whitaker‘s “If I Know You,” Josh Kerr and Lauren Weintraub‘s “NYC” and Brian Falduto and Presidio‘s “Skip The Step (Alternate Version).”

Keep scrolling to see our list, and be sure to let us know which track you like the best by either leaving a reaction at the bottom of the post, or by sending us a DM at @celebsecretscountry.
